- Skagerak Energi focuses on risk management and internal control in all parts of the value chain
- A full review of risk factors is conducted once a year, with reports presented to the board twice a year
- A plan is created to reduce risks or increase the likelihood of achieving gains
- Risk assessment is mandatory for larger projects
- Board members are compensated according to the general meeting's decision, with no performance-based bonuses
- Leading employees' compensation is determined by the CEO and approved by the board
- Skagerak Energi prioritizes communication, dialogue, and collaboration with stakeholders
- Shareholder agreement includes provisions for pre-emptive rights and restrictions on ownership
- Deloitte AS is the chosen auditor for Skagerak Energi, attending the board meeting for final approval of the annual financial statements
- The auditor's compensation is disclosed in note 8 of the financial statements
